A reference for scientific authorship

This page contains a curated and structured collection of freely available resources. If you are new to scientific authorship, explore the topics sequentially because each topic provides a foundation for the next one. The resources within each topic are not in any particular order. Look at the resource titles and click on the ones you find interesting. Then you may want to search online to find similar resources. —Ravi Murugesan

If you're just starting out to read journal papers (or think back to the time you did), there's a good chance of experiencing an imposter syndrome. But reading papers is a skill and can be learnt. And only by extensive reading of previous research in your area will you be in a strong position to chart a direction for your own research.
